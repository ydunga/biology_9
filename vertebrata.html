Vertebrata

Animals belonging to this group have a true vertebral column, allowing for advanced movement.

All chordates have the following feautures:

  1. Have a notochord
  2. Have a dorsal(located at the back) nerve cord
  3. are triploblastic
  4. Have paired gill pouches
  5. are coelmate (possess body cavity within which internal organs are suspended)

Based on these characteristics vertebrates can be grouped into the following six categories.

Cyclostomata

Cyclostomes are jawless vertebrates.

They have elongated bodies with slimy skin and are scaleless.

Pisces

Pisces are exclusively aquatic animals (fish).

Their skin is covered in scales and their has a streamlined shape, with a muscular tail used for movement.

Fish are cold blooded animals i.e they cannot maintain a constant body temperature. Instead their body temperature fluctuates with the external environment.

Unlike human beings, fish have only two chambers in their heart.

Fishes can differ in terms of how boney they are or how much cartilage they have in their skeletons.

Amphibians

The class of amphibians includes about 5000 species.

Toads, frogs, newts and salamanders make up the bulk of amphibian species.

Amphibians do not have scales like fish, instead they have mucus glands in their skin.

Amphibians are also cold blooded.

They have a three chambered heart and respiration is performed either through gills or lungs.

Amphibians are unique because they can be found both in water and on land.

Amphibians reproduce through external fertilization. For example, in frogs, the female frog releases egss in the water. The male frog then releases sperm to fertilize them.

Reptilia

Common reptiles include snakes, lizards and crocodiles.

Reptiles are also cold blooded and have scaled on their body.

Unlike pisces, respiration is done through lungs.

Most reptiles have three chambered hearts, however crocodiles have four chambered hearts.

Unlike amphibians, fertilization in reptiles is internal.

Aves/birds

There are about 10,000 species of birds. They are found in a wide variety of environments.

Birds are warm blooded animals and have a four chambered heart.

Instead of scales they have feathers.

The body of a bird is characterized by their limbs, which have adapted over years to accomodate the muscle activity needed for flying.

Birds have a well developed circulatory system, with complete seperation of oxygenated and deoxygenated blood.

Fertilization in birds is internal. Post fertilization they lay eggs, which hatch once the incubation process is over.

Mammalia

Mammals are considered the animal group with the most sophisticated body structure.

Within mammals, we have marsupials - who give birth to live but underdeveloped young ones and placentals who give birth to live well developed young offspring.

Instead of scales or feather, mammals have hair on their body. Hair helps mammals protect against sunlight and provides insulation.

Mammals are warm blooded animals and have a well defined circulatory system.

Mammals also have entirely internal fertilization.

This brings us to the end of our study of classification of animals.
